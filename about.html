Mission & Global Impact

mercuri.world is a global, student-led organization dedicated to advancing mental health awareness through education, innovation, and community action. Our mission is to make mental health knowledge accessible by blending psychology, neuroscience, and digital technology into meaningful initiatives that empower students. Across our worldwide chapters, members develop projects, lead workshops, conduct research, and create digital tools designed to support student well-being. With support from organizations such as GitHub Education and Google.org, mercuri.world continues to grow as a credible force for change, inspiring young people to make a positive impact in their schools and communities. Our global network works toward a shared vision: a world where every student has the support, resources, and understanding they need to thrive.

Our Story

mercuri.world began as an idea within the Hack Foundation, a nonprofit created by students and alumni from institutions such as Harvard and MIT. What started as a small initiative quickly grew into a global community once its founders recognized how deeply students needed accessible, science-backed mental health education. From the beginning, mercuri.world has been shaped by the belief that young people can lead meaningful change when given the tools, knowledge, and opportunities to do so. Over time, the club expanded into a structured international program with dedicated committees, leadership roles, research efforts, educational events, and digital innovation projects. Today, mercuri.world continues to evolve through the passion of its members — students who are committed to improving mental health support in their schools, communities, and beyond. The organization remains grounded in its original vision: to empower students everywhere to learn, create, and advocate for a healthier future.
